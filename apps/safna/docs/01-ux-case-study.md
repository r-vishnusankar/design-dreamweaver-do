# UX Case Study — From Her Heart

## Overview

**Client:** Safna Latheef (Bride)  
**Companion Site:** [Jithin Abdulsalam — Groom Invitation](https://jithin-wedding-invitation.vercel.app)  
**Theme:** From Her Heart  
**Event:** Wedding Reception — 16 August 2026

## Problem Statement

The groom already has a premium digital invitation. The bride needs her own chapter — not a feminine copy, but a complementary narrative that feels like reading two volumes of the same love story.

## Design Philosophy

| Groom's Site | Bride's Site |
|---|---|
| Structured, informational | Story-driven, emotional |
| Event-centric hero | Portrait-centric hero |
| Direct invitation tone | Personal letter tone |
| Shared palette family | Ivory, sage, champagne warmth |

Both sites share: Islamic greeting, wedding facts, family details, venue, countdown — ensuring guests get consistent information while experiencing distinct emotional journeys.

## Target Audience

- Extended family across Kerala (Palakkad ↔ Thrissur)
- Friends and colleagues
- International guests needing clear date/venue info
- Mobile-first users (primary device in India)

## Success Metrics

- Time on site > 2 minutes (engagement with story)
- Blessings submissions
- Cross-link clicks to groom's site
- Calendar/directions actions

## Key UX Decisions

1. **"Begin the Experience" gate** — Respects no-autoplay; sets premium tone
2. **Chapter-based scroll** — Editorial magazine flow vs. flat sections
3. **Opening animation** — Emotional hook before information
4. **Cross-link to groom** — Explicit "two chapters" narrative in Their Story
5. **Night mode after sunset** — Subtle, respectful ambiance shift

## Competitive Reference

The groom site establishes trust and clarity. The bride site adds intimacy, journey, and personal voice — together they form a complete digital wedding suite.
